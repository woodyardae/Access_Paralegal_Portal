How do I force a document to jump to the top of a massive queue?

Category: Productivity & Tips • Last Updated May 2026

When handling cases with **hundreds of PDFs**, dragging-and-dropping an individual exhibit up 300 rows can be tedious. We designed a professional shortcut tool exactly for this scenario:

The "Double-Click Index Jump"

  1. Locate the PDF exhibit you wish to move.
  2. **Double-click** its current number in the **`#`** (first) column.
  3. A small text entry box will instantly appear inside the cell.
  4. Type the number you want this document to jump to (e.g., type `1` to move it to the very top).
  5. Press Enter.

The entire queue automatically splits open, shifts other exhibits downward, and drops the selected document exactly at your typed destination in milliseconds.

⚡ Pro-Tip: You can also hold `Ctrl` or `Shift` on your keyboard to select *multiple* files at once, and then click the "Move Up" or "Move Down" nudging arrow buttons in the toolbar for batch ordering!
