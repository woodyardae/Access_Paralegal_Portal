Why did my Bates number not reset to 1 when I opened the app today?

Category: Bates Stamping • Last Updated May 2026

This is one of our premier **Malpractice Prevention** features.

In most standard PDF programs, if you stamp a sequence today (e.g., `SMITH_001` to `SMITH_050`), and then come back tomorrow to stamp supplementary exhibits, the software defaults back to `1`. If you aren't paying close attention, you might accidentally stamp `SMITH_001` again. In legal proceedings, duplicating a Bates number is a severe error that disrupts record admissibility.

Introducing the Persistent Encrypted Ledger

Access Paralegal Suite contains an embedded, **AES-256 encrypted Bates Registry database**. Whenever you successfully flatten a merged bundle, the software automatically logs the **Prefix** used and the **highest ending index number** you reached.

The next time you type `SMITH` into the Bates Prefix field—whether that is 5 minutes later or 6 months later—the software instantly searches its secure local database and **auto-populates the Starting Index to the next logical number** (e.g., `51`).

💡 Safe by Design: You never have to keep a manual notepad tracking what number you stopped on for each client case. The application safely handles that ledger data 100% locally inside your case vault!
